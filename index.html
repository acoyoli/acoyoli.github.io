About Me

I have a doctorate in Mathematics from Tufts University in Massachusetts, USA. Broadly speaking, my research focuses on inverse problems. I am particularly interested in problems related to analytic tomography, computerized tomography and integral geometry. I obtained my masters in Mathematics, with a substantive focus in Applied Mathematics, from Tufts University. Prior to Tufts, I obtained a B.Sc. in Applied Mathematics from el Instituto Tecnológico Autonomo de México (ITAM) in Mexico City, Mexico.

Throughout my career, I have acquired useful tools and skills for solving real life problems. These include programming languages such as C, Python, Matlab, R, Java, as well as mathematical modeling, image processing, machine learning, data simulation, statistics and numerical optimization. I have also had the privilege to create my own courses in Data Science and Computational Mathematics, teach and tutor undergraduates and masters students.

Outside academia, I enjoy tinkering with electronics, writing software, reading fiction and non-fiction, and making music and beer.
